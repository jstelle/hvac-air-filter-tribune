how Often Should I Have My Hvac System Serviced?

Regular maintenance is the key to keeping your HVAC system running smoothly. In the same way that your car needs oil changes and tune-ups to keep it going, so does your HVAC system. A regular HVAC maintenance schedule helps to identify potential problems before they become full-blown issues and can save you money in the long run by preventing more expensive repairs in the future. A regular HVAC maintenance schedule not only helps to prevent breakdowns and costly repairs, but also extends the lifespan of your HVAC system. A dirty or clogged HVAC system can reduce the efficiency of your air conditioning unit by up to 25%. HVAC maintenance can help you avoid these problems by cleaning out your system’s pipes, filters and other components to remove dirt and debris buildup.A good HVAC maintenance plan will help you identify problems before they become serious issues and will help you to extend the life of your HVAC system in the process.

Change your filters regularly

One of the most important parts of a regular HVAC maintenance schedule is changing your air filter. Air filters play an important role in keeping your HVAC system clean and free of debris. They trap dirt and dust that would otherwise be released into the air. Over time, these contaminants can cause a buildup in your system that can lead to reduced performance and efficiency. By regularly changing your air filter, you can keep your system clean and avoid these issues. There are a number of different types of filters you can use in your HVAC system, but a basic dust-only filter is a good place to start. Dust-only filters should be changed every one to two weeks, depending on the usage of your home.Dust-only filters should be changed more frequently if you have pets or if you live in a dusty area.

Check your fuses and circuit breakers

Another part of an HVAC maintenance schedule that is often overlooked is checking your fuses and circuit breakers. Fuses and circuit breakers are components found in your HVAC system that help to protect your home from overheating and other electrical issues. When these components fail, they can prevent your HVAC system from working properly. Fuses are generally found in the main panels of your home, while circuit breakers are often found in the main electrical panels. When these components fail, it’s a good idea to replace them. This will ensure that your HVAC system is properly protected from electrical issues and will keep it running efficiently.

Check your air ducts

In addition to checking your fuses and circuit breakers, it’s a good idea to check your air ducts. Air ducts are the pipes that carry air from your HVAC system to each room of your home. They can become clogged with debris and dirt over time, which can reduce the flow of air through your HVAC system. This can lead to reduced performance, higher utility bills and other problems. To check your air ducts, you can use a camera to inspect the inside of your ductwork. You can also use a vacuum to remove any debris or dirt that has built up in your ductwork.

Change your HVAC system’s oil

Another important part of an HVAC maintenance schedule is changing your HVAC system’s oil. HVAC systems use oil to lubricate their moving parts. Over time, the oil in your system will break down and need to be replaced. This can help to prevent issues with your system and extend its lifespan. Oil changes are typically recommended once every 12 months, or every 6000 hours of use. If you’re particularly concerned about the performance of your HVAC system, you can do a more frequent oil change.

Clean out your condensate pan

Depending on where your HVAC system is located, you may have a condensate pan. This is the container that collects water in your HVAC system that condenses from the air as it cools. If this water is left uncleaned, it can lead to several issues, including corrosion and mold. To clean your condensate pan, you can use a garden hose with a nozzle attachment to direct the water outside. You can also use a cleaning solution to help to remove any debris or scum that has built up in the pan.

Conclusion

When it comes to your HVAC system, it’s important to keep up with regular maintenance. By checking your fuses and circuit breakers, air ducts, oil and condensate pan, you can prevent issues and extend the life of your HVAC system. A regular maintenance schedule is an important part of keeping your HVAC system running efficiently and safely. With the right maintenance program, you can help to avoid breakdowns and costly repairs in the future. A good HVAC maintenance plan will help you identify potential problems before they become full-blown issues and will help you to extend the life of your HVAC system.
