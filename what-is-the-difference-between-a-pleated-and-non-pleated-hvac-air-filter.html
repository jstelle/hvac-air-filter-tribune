What Is the Difference Between a Pleated and Non-Pleated Hvac Air Filter?

Pleated and non-pleated HVAC filters are two of the most common types of filters used in air conditioning systems. What are the benefits of a pleated HVAC filter?

Pleated filters are designed to trap larger particles, like dust and pollen, and have a large surface area. A pleated filter has many folds and pockets that trap air and dust particles. The more surface area a filter has, the more efficiently it can trap particles. A pleated filter can trap as much as 99.9% of all particles as small as 0.3 microns. A pleated filter is typically made out of polyester or polyester-nitrile, which is an environmentally-friendly synthetic material.Pleated filters are also reusable and can be easily cleaned to remove dirt and dust build-up. The more surface area a filter has, the longer it can last.

What are the benefits of a non-pleated HVAC filter?

Non-pleated filters are typically made out of paper or polyester-wool. A non-pleated filter has a small surface area and is generally used for removing very small particles like dust and pollen. A non-pleated HVAC filter is not as effective at trapping larger particles like hair and other debris. A non-pleated filter can trap as much as 99% of all particles as small as 0.3 microns.Non-pleated filters are typically less expensive than pleated filters and can be used for both the heating and cooling system in your home.
